Accurate Profiling

The accuracy of the profile data depends on the accuracy of the timers used. The profiling framework in the JDE Commons (used by DocMaker) includes timers called stop-watches. There are a number of different stop-watch implementations that use different timing mechanisms depending the version and vendor of the JRE used; the most accurate one that can be supported by the JRE will be selected automatically at run-time. Regardless of the accuracy of the stop-watch used, all of the profiles report times in nanoseconds.

For accurate profiling, a JRE 1.5 (or later) should be used, but profiling under the Sun Microsystems 1.4.2 (or later) JRE is also quite accurate. Both of these JREs provide sub-microsecond accuracy and can accurately measure the elapsed time of very fast operations, such as simple templates in a stylesheet. Timing facilities in earlier JREs or in the IBM 1.4.2 JRE are not as accurate and have only millisecond accuracy or worse. This is not accurate enough for profiling XSLT stylesheets, but may suffice when profiling the more lengthy tasks of an Ant script.

The jde-commons-ext.jar file supplied with DocMaker is a special build of the JDE Commons that includes all of the stop-watch implementations, ensuring that the best possible implementation will be selected for you automatically. While the jde-commons.jar file supplied with the Cúram JDE could be used when profiling, it may have been created with a JRE that could not compile all of the stop-watches and may not be able to support accurate profiling under any JRE.
