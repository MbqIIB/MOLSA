Database Table: AttributedEvidence

Table Description
Attributed Evidence: The period during which an evidence record is used in eligibility determination on a particular case.

Part of evidence maintenance requires users to capture and maintain business dates for evidence e.g. the loan drawdown date, last date of employment, date of admission to hospital, date of release from hospital etc. These dates will influence the period of applicability for the piece of evidence but other factors may also apply, e.g. organization business rules or program specific business rules in relation to lowest level of granularity for period of applicability, aligning to full weeks, months or quarters. Using temporal evidence solution the user takes responsibility for the capture and maintenance of the business dates only and the system uses these in combination with the business rules to calculate the period of applicability for the evidence i.e. the attribution period.

Note that separating the attribution period from the piece of evidence allows for a different attribution period to be calculated for each different product delivery case using this piece of evidence, as the business rules used in calculating the attribution period may be product specific.

Example

Income evidence is captured on an Integrated Case and used by both Income Support and Carers Allowance products on the Integrated Case. Business rules exist that all income support cases are delivered weekly on Monday's in advance, carers allowance cases are delivered Tuesday's in arrears. Participant Linda Smith has claimed for both income support and carers allowance and these are actively in payment on the system. Linda Smith informs the organization of new income. Once this income evidence is activated, two attribution periods will be calculated by the system for this piece of evidence; one based on income support attribution rules and one based on carers allowance rules.
